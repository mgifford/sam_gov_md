# Z--GAOA - National Elk Refuge Electrical Repairs

- Agency: INTERIOR, DEPARTMENT OF THE
- Type: Presolicitation
- Posted: 2026-03-23 08:55:03.789-04
- Solicitation Number: 140FGA26R0013

## Summary

This is a Synopsis/Pre-Solicitation notice. The final solicitation package will follow. This opportunity is only available to Small Businesses. The U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service Great American Outdoors Act (GAOA) Construction Team has a requirement for the following: The U.S. Fish and Wildlife staff have identified 18 structures on the Elk Refuge Compound that need electrical repairs. The structures that need electrical repairs are the following: Administrative Building, New Shop Building with 3-Vehicle Bays, Quarters #1, Quarters #3, Quarters #9, Quarters #12, Quarters #20, Quarters #128, Quarters #147, Miller House Garage, Duplex Quarters 14538 A/B, Horse Barn, 4-Car Garage Bunkroom, Equipment Storage Building, Log Horse Barn, Blacksmith Shop, Winter Pasture Horse Shelter, and Miller Residence. The National Elk Refuge is a Wildlife Refuge located in Jackson Hole, Wyoming. It was created in 1912 to protect habitat and provide sanctuary for one of the largest elk herds. With a total of 24,700 acres, the refuge borders the town of Jackson Wyoming on the southwest, Bridger-Teton National Forest on the east and Grand Teton National Park on the north. It is home to an average of 7,500 elk each winter. This requirement is being procured in accordance with FAR Part 15, Contracting by Negotiation. This will be a Firm Fixed Priced Contract and Award will be based on Lowest Price Technically Acceptable. The complete Solicitation will be posted to https://sam.gov/ under Contract Opportunities on or about April 9, 2026. Interested contractors must be registered in SAM (System for Award Management, www.sam.gov) and complete Online Representations and Certifications. A pre-proposal site visit and instructions will also be announced with the solicitation. The NAICS code is 238210 - Electrical Contractors and Other Wiring Installation Contractors and the size standard is $19 million. The project magnitude is between $250,000 and $500,000. The work will commence within 10 days of Notice to Proceed and be completed by the agreed upon date at the time of award. Bonding Requirements: 20% Bid Bond, 100% Payment Bond, 100% Performance Bond Direct questions in writing via email ONLY to Samantha LaGue at Samantha_LaGue@ios.doi.gov
