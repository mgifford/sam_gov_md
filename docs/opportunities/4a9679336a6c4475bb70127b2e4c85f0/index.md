2.0 SCOPE Electroencephalography uses electrodes placed on a patient's scalp to measure, amplify, filter, and record the electrical signals (EEG signals) generated by the brain and subsequently display the acquired patient data in digital wave forms. There are three Neuroiagnostic procedures that Electroencephalographic systems are used for, Electroencephalography (EEG), Intraoperative Monitoring (IONM), Long-Term Monitoring (LTM). The first Neurodiagnostic procedure, EEG is used in areas such Outpatient Labs, Inpatient Wards, and ICU settings. It observes and diagnoses a range of neurologic conditions, including epilepsy. It can also be employed to evaluate psychiatric disorders and differentiate them from neurologic conditions. IONM has the capability to monitor the functional integrity of neural structures during surgeries such as Carotid Endarterectomy, Corticography, Brain Mapping, Stimulation, Aneurysm Clipping, and the effects of anesthesia on the brain during these procedures. LTM is the collection of EEG data over a period of time. These recordings include Ambulatory EEG (aEEG), which is used for recording at the Veteran s residence. Continuous EEG (cEEG) is used for inpatient video monitoring greater than 2 hours. Prolonged EEG (pEEG) monitoring is used for outpatient video monitoring greater than 2 hours. Video EEG (vEEG) is an elective EEG admission for prolonged video EEG recording which is performed in an Epilepsy Monitoring Unit (EMU). An EMU is a specialized inpatient unit designed to evaluate, diagnose, and treat seizures. The ability to record and monitor non-invasive scalp electrode EEG, intracranial EEG (Grid and Strip), and invasive stereotactic depth electrodes (sEEG) is the gold standard using state-of-the art continuous video electroencephalogram (vEEG) monitoring technology.
