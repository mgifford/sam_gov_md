Crescenz VA Medical Center CT Injection systems Introduction: The Computed Tomography (CT) department is critical to our diagnostic imaging services, providing essential support for patient care. The department relies heavily on CT Contrast injectors to ensure accurate and efficient procedures. However, the two CT Contrast Injectors currently in use have reached their end-of-life statute, making their replacement an urgent necessity. To ensure continuity and quality of our CT imaging services, we propose the procurement of two new CT Contrast Injection Systems. The new injection systems must include ü Injection unit the core component of the system that houses the mechanism for dispensing the contrast media. It will have a programmable setting to control the rate, Cordless Mobility Simple snap-in patient line auto primes and is ready for the next patient in under 20 seconds. ü Multi patient set-up. ü Contrast versatility & Data Capture. ü Visual Indicators. ü Air Detection and Removal. ü Fluid Delivery Method. ü Flow rate: 0.1-10mL/sec in 0.1 mL increments. ü Volume 1mL to Syringe Capacity in 1mL increments. ü Programmable Pressure Limit (psI/kPa): 150mL and 200mL Syringe: Choice of 50/345, 100/689; 150/1034, 200/1379, 225/1551, 250/1724, 300/2068, 325/2241. ü Scan reminders: 0-300 Seconds (5 minutes) in 1 second increments. ü Pause: 1-900 Seconds (15 minutes) in 1 Second Increments. ü Hold: Maximum HOLD Time is 20 minutes. ü Maximum Number of Phases: 6. ü Syringes (Volume capacity): 150mL or 200mL Sterile Disposable Syringe. ü Fluid Loading Contrast Media: 150 mL or 200 mL Imaging Bulk Pack presentation. ü Day Set Two X 200 mL reservoirs. ü Dual Flow: Simultaneous injection of Contrast tubing with T-connector, 2 small spikes ü Installation Deliverables 1) Delivery and installation of two state-of-the-art CT Contrast injection systems, each equipped with advanced technology to ensure
