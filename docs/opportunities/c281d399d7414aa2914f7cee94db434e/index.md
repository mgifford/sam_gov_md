# USCGC OBION BACKFLOW REPAIR

- Agency: HOMELAND SECURITY, DEPARTMENT OF
- Type: Combined Synopsis/Solicitation
- Posted: 2026-03-25 12:51:16.392-04
- Solicitation Number: 70Z02926QGALV0010

## Summary

Solicitation 70Z02926QGALV0010 is extended to 4/3/2026 to allow for cutter availability. CGC Obion Repair Backflow Preventers Piping NAICS: 238220 (Plumbing, heating and air conditioning) PSC: J045 United States Coast Guard Cutter Obion is accepting proposals for qualified individuals or firms, to perform plumbing repairs and renewal at the shore side detachment facility in Owensboro, KY. All work being performed must be up to state and federal code. Should any permits need to be required to perform this project, the contractor shall acquire all necessary permits for the project and provide all labor, tools, equipment and materials to complete the project. Furnished items shall include all relevant materials to remove existing piping, renew piping, fittings, insulation, anchors, and back-flow preventers, etc. location of service: USCGC Obion 3301 E. 4th street Owensboro, KY 42303 This is a combined synopsis/solicitation. All work is to be completed in accordance with the scope of work. This announcement constitutes only the solicitation; quotes are being requested, and no other written solicitation will not be issued. This is a Small Business Set-aside, the applicable NAICS code is 238220 and small business size standard is 19M. This solicitation will result in a Firm-Fixed Price Construction Contract awarded to the offeror with the Lowest Price Technically Acceptable. Offerors must be actively registered in System for Award Management - https://www.sam.gov. All work is to be completed in accordance with the SOW. This will be a firm-fixed priced purchase order awarded to the lowest reasonable price technically acceptable bidder. This is set aside 100% for small business. All contractors must be actively registered in SAM.gov and must provide representations prescribed in clause 52.236-1 in Section III and see attachment B to be considered. The Contractor shall submit, simultaneously with the schedule of costs required by the Payments under Fixed-Price Construction Contracts clause of the contract, a statement designating the portions of contract work to be performed with the Contractor's own forces. The approved schedule of costs will be used in determining the value of a work activity/event, or portions thereof, of the work for the purpose of this article. In establishing the percent of work to be performed by the Contractor. Cost of material and equipment installed by such labor may be included. The work by the Contractor's executive, administrative and clerical forces shall be excluded in establishing compliance with the requirements of this clause. FAR 52.237-1 -- Site Visit (Apr 1984). Offerors or quoters are urged and expected to inspect the site where services are to be performed and to satisfy themselves regarding all general and local conditions that may affect the cost of contract performance, to the extent that the information is reasonably obtainable. In no event shall failure to inspect the site constitute grounds for a claim after contract award. Arrangements for mandatory site visits may be made by contacting: MKC Corey Walters Corey.a.walters@uscg.mil 270-314-8761 Submit quotes to: Jonathan.d.thomas@uscg.mil and Lynn.P.Charles2@uscg.mil NAICS: 238220 PSC: J045
