# Hand Sanitizer Recycling/Disposal

- Agency: JUSTICE, DEPARTMENT OF
- Type: Solicitation
- Posted: 2026-04-13 10:52:00.381-04
- Solicitation Number: 15F06726Q0000094

## Summary

The Government hereby cancels Solicitation No. 15F06726Q0000094 for the recycling/disposal of bulk hand sanitizer. This requirement is no longer needed at this time. As a result, the solicitation is cancelled in its entirety and no award will be made. The Government appreciates your interest in this opportunity and the time and effort invested in preparing a quotation. We encourage your participation in future requirements. Quantity is updated to reflect the following - this has also been updated on the Answer sheet: As of 4/3/2026: There are 3 pallets. Each pallet consists of 14 boxes stacked 3 high for a total of 168 boxes. Each box holds 12 bottles for a total of 2016 bottles. **UPDATE** Answer to questions have been uploaded. FAR 52.219-14 Subcontracting Limitations Defined Offerors are advised that compliance with the Limitations on Subcontracting requirement will be evaluated consistent with applicable regulations and controlling legal interpretations. For purposes of this procurement, the Government will assess compliance based on the percentage of the cost of contract performance incurred for personnel (for services contracts), as required by FAR 52.219-14 and implementing SBA regulations. Offerors are specifically cautioned that: The subcontracting limitation applies to the performance of the primary and vital requirements of the contract, not to administrative or incidental functions. Activities such as invoicing, billing, contract administration, general management, or other back-office support functions do not constitute performance of the contract’s primary requirements. Accordingly, such back-office or administrative functions will not be credited toward meeting the required percentage of work that must be performed by the prime contractor (or similarly situated entities, if applicable). The Government will evaluate compliance based on the extent to which the prime contractor is performing the core labor categories and substantive technical requirements identified in the Performance Work Statement (PWS) or Statement of Work (SOW). Failure to demonstrate compliance with these requirements may render a proposal ineligible for award or may result in a determination of noncompliance during contract performance. For the avoidance of doubt, merely retaining functions such as contract billing or administrative oversight, while subcontracting the substantive technical work, does not satisfy the limitations on subcontracting requirement. After COVID-19, the FBI was left with a surplus of expired hand sanitizer. Due to the high alcohol content, this material must be handled and recycled/disposed correctly according to EPA and DOT regulations. The Contractor shall provide all services necessary to recycle or dispose of expired or unused hand sanitizer. Quantity is as follows: 82 full boxes on five pallets. Each box holds 12 bottles, approximately 2184 bottles in total. Each bottle is 750ml. The work includes pickup, transport, recycling/disposal, and provision of documentation in accordance with applicable Federal, State, and local regulations. Questions are due 4/2/26 no later than 4pm EST. Questions must be submitted via email to mgolicz@fbi.gov via the questions Excel template.
