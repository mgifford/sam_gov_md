# Utility Building Staircase

- Agency: STATE, DEPARTMENT OF
- Type: Presolicitation
- Posted: 2026-03-31 02:07:57.49-04
- Solicitation Number: PR15927128

## Summary

EXPRESSION OF INTEREST The General Services Office (GSO) US Mission South Africa seeks to enter into a contract with qualified, reliable firms with sufficient resources, financing, and specific work experience related to STRUCTURAL STEEL construction, for delivering and performing of services at US Consulate located on 1 Sandton Drive, Sandhurst, Johannesburg. The scope of work includes but not limited to the following. Establishment of site for installation and removal thereof on completion Consulate Security Clearance on all contractors’ personnel to execute work on premises. Associated preparation, clearing, protection, safety, and reinstatement on completion. Adherence to OSH Act and health and safety compliance Temporary protection of and support to existing equipment and property Removal or relocation of existing equipment, electrical cabling, and DB’s where indicated Measurement, submission of shop drawings, and manufacturing of steel staircase Installation of temporary access to the bottom of the work area. Manufacture, delivery, and installation of steel staircase including crane facilities Commission and handover of site A compulsory site meeting will be held at 1 Sandton Drive, Sandhurst, Johannesburg on Wednesday, 13 May 2026 @ 10h30 am during which brief the date and time of tender closure will be confirmed as well as the full Scope of Work and Tender documents be provided. All interested contractors must confirm their attendance no later than COB (17H00), Friday, 8 May 2026 via email to Monica Nagley (nagleym@state.gov), Didi Thipe (thipeda@state.gov) and Adriaan Joubert (joubertad@state.gov) Kindly provide the following on your confirmation of attendance. Reference Number Utility Building Staircase – Site Visit - PR15927128 Full name of attendee with contact number (preferably only one representative per Company). Provide proof of ID at the site meeting. Please ensure you get an e-mail receipt acknowledgement from us in return to ensure you are added onto the attendee list. The following important requirements are applicable Only Contractors with specific structural steel construction experience with value above R 0.5 million may attend the site-meeting. Contractors are requested to bring their own measuring equipment to the Site Meeting. Contractors will be required to provide proof and references of previous projects completed. Any Proposals received without this proof, will not be considered. Structural Steel Manufacturing will not be allowed to be sub-contracted. The successful bidder must have a company that specialises in the manufacturing and installation of structural steel, with a least 10 years track record in the same industry. U.S Embassy payment terms: i) The US Embassy will make no provision for fluctuations in price or exchange rates after the order has been placed. If your price is dependent on these fluctuations, you MUST make provision for possible increases in your quoted price. ii) The U.S Embassy does not authorize Advance (deposit) payments. Payment will be made via EFT into the company’s bank account, within 30 days of delivery of goods/services and receipt of proper Invoice. iv) By submitting a Quotation, you are agreeing to waiver any payment terms that may be in contrast with the above. Payments for work completed will be done as follows. Claim 1 as measured and certified on delivery to site of work completed and payable within 30 days of submission. Claim 2 as measured and certified on 100 % completion of work and payable within 30 days of submission. Compulsory SAM Registration All tenderers must register with SAM - System for Award Management, by the time of the award, as part of the tender process. Instructions are available on the U.S Embassy South Africa website. NO TENDERS WILL BE ACCEPTED WITHOUT PROOF OF REGISTRATION. Once registered please attach the DUNS and NCAGE number as proof to the Tender document. U.S. National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) Section 889(a)(1)(B) The US Government’s Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) has been updated with new clauses and provisions, per the National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) Section 889(a)(1)(B), that impact the U.S. Government entering new or extending current contracts, purchase orders, task/delivery orders, purchase card orders, petty cash purchases, Blanket Purchase Agreement or any other acquisition method. These newly introduced FAR clauses will have impact on any acquisition made by the U.S. Government no matter the acquisition value. We kindly request you to read FAR clause 52.204-25 (PROHIBITION ON CONTRACTING FOR CERTAIN TELECOMMUNICATIONS AND VIDEO SURVEILLANCE SERVICES OR EQUIPMENT (AUG 2020)) carefully and complete the following FAR Provision 52.204-24 (REPRESENTATION REGARDING CERTAIN TELECOMMUNICATIONS AND VIDEO SURVEILLANCE SERVICES OR EQUIPMENT (AUG 2020)) and 52.204-26 (COVERED TELECOMMUNICATIONS EQUIPMENT OR SERVICES-REPRESENTATION (DEC 2019)) on the enclosed form. Additional information about covered telecommunication equipment or services you may find in FAR subpart 4.21 on the following internet site: https://www.acquisition.gov/content/part-4-administrative-and-information-matters#id19899A00RSK There are several terms and phrases in Section 889 with which you must be familiar. These are described in 52.204.25(a). Exclusions are described in clause 52.204.25(c). Complete page 1 and tick the applicable boxes on pages 2 and 4.
