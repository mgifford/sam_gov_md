# Relocate Schofield Barracks Deep Wells Above Ground, Building 1580, Schofield Barracks, Oahu, Hawaii

- Posted: 2026-04-20 22:12:12.836-04

## Summary

This announcement constitutes a Pre-Solicitation notice (SYNOPSIS) in accordance with FAR Subpart 5.2. The U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, Honolulu District hereby announces its intent to issue a solicitation entitled, ?Relocate Schofield Barracks Deep Wells Above Ground, Building 1580, Schofield Barracks, Oahu, Hawaii? as described within this announcement. This project is a Design-Bid-Build solicitation, utilizing Best Value Trade Off (BVTO) procedures in accordance with FAR Part 15. The Government intends to solicit this requirement as unrestricted procurement. The North American Industry Classification code (NAICS) for this requirement is 237110. The Small Business size standard for this NAICS code is $45,000,000 annual average revenue. The Government intends to award a firm-fixed-price construction contract as a result of this solicitation. Interested Offerors should register as a Vendor in Procurement Integrated Enterprise Environment (PIEE) in order to participate in the upcoming solicitation once issued. Please review the vendor registration instructions at: https://piee.eb.mil/xhtml/unauth/web/homepage/vendorGettingStartedHelp.xhtml.Project Description: Drill four wells ? estimated to be approximately 850 feet deep and 26-28 inches in diameter (exact depths and dimensions to be determined by design). Existing wells and pumps located below ground and are to remain on-line until new wells can manage peak demand of Schofield in June ? September with consideration for the largest pump to be on stand-by. Motors to be located above ground. Each well, pump, motor sized to deliver a minimum of 2,000 gallons per minute. Roof structure (overhead cover/canopy only) required to protect the motors from the elements. The estimated period of performance is 1,734 calendar days for Base. The Government anticipates the acquisition will be best value trade off based on the following criteria: 1) Past Performance; 2) Technical Approach; 3) Small Business Participation Plan; 4) Bonding Capability; and 5) Price. Project magnitude, in accordance with DFARS 236.204, is between $25M-$100M.In accordance with FAR Subpart 22.5, Use of Project Labor Agreements for Federal Construction Projects, FAR Provision 52.222-33 and FAR Clause 52.222-34 will apply to this requirement. An organized site visit will be conducted at a date and time that is to be determined. It is the Offeror?s responsibility to check for any posted changes to the synopsis and solicitation. Solicitation documents will not be issued on compact disc (CD) unless the Government determines that it is necessary. Offerors must have and/or maintain active registration with the System Award Management (SAM) website. To register in SAM, got to https://www.sam.gov. All Offerors are encouraged to visit https://www.sam.gov website to view other business opportunities.
