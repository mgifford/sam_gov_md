# Repair and Refurbishment of Soffit and Eaves – Building 3760, USCG Station Pensacola

## Summary

This is a combined synopsis/solicitation for construction services prepared in accordance with the format in FAR Part 36, as supplemented with additional information included in this notice. This announcement constitutes the only solicitation; bids are being requested, and no other written solicitation will be issued. Instructions to bidders, evaluation criteria, and all applicable FAR Provisions and Clauses are attached. Solicitation Number: 70Z02926BNEWO000004 SF-1442 The solicitation document and incorporated provisions and clauses, including Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) Clauses and Department of Labor Wage Rates for Construction, are included and applicable. General Decision Number: FL20250203 Where Federal Acquisition Regulation clause language is inconsistent with FAR Part 36, Legal Effect of Bids, that language is hereby tailored in accordance with FAR Part 36 to be consistent with an invitation for bids. This tailoring includes but is not limited to the following: In accordance with FAR Part 36, a bid is not a quotation, and any clause provision to the contrary is hereby modified to be consistent with FAR Part 36. Where the word "quote/quoter" or a derivative of that word appears, it is changed to "bid/bidder" or a derivative of that word. Set-Aside: 100% Small Business NAICS Code: 236220 PSC Code: Z2AA This solicitation will result in a Firm-Fixed Price Construction Contract. Award will be made to the lowest price, technically acceptable, responsible bidder. The evaluation factors are: Price Technical Acceptability Past Performance Active SAM.gov registration is required. Project Scope: The United States Coast Guard is seeking bids from qualified contractors to perform comprehensive repairs and refurbishment of the soffit and eaves of Building 3760 at USCG Station Pensacola, 21 Slemmer Ave, Pensacola, FL 32508, in accordance with the attached Statement of Work. Work includes: Repairing or replacing damaged or loose tongue-and-groove wood sections in the eaves and soffits with materials matching the existing style and quality. Sanding, staining, and sealing all wood surfaces to protect against weather damage and decay. Removing rust and repainting the structural support beam to its original white color. Removing and replacing all existing 16”x8” and 6”x8” soffit vents with new, equivalent white vents. Providing and removing a dumpster for debris, and ensuring proper disposal in accordance with federal, state, and local environmental regulations. Protecting government property and equipment in or near the work area. Adhering to strict security, safety, and environmental protocols on the operational military installation. Coordinating work schedules to minimize disruption to building operations. Complying with all applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations, including the Endangered Species Act (ESA), Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA), and others as applicable. Performance Period: 120 calendar days from Notice to Proceed. Site Visit: FAR 52.236-27 – Site Visit (Construction). Bidders are urged and expected to inspect the site where work will be performed and to satisfy themselves regarding all general and local conditions that may affect the cost of contract performance, to the extent that the information is reasonably obtainable. In no event shall failure to inspect the site constitute grounds for a claim after contract award. Site visits must be scheduled with the designated point of contact prior to submitting a bid.
