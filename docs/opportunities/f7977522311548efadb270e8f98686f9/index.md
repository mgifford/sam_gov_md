CONTRACTING WITHOUT PROVIDING FOR FULL OR OPEN COMPETITION (INCLUDING BRAND-NAME) DETERMINATION The Government’s determination to award this requirement on a non-competitive basis is based on the unique technical requirements of the acquisition and the results of market research conducted in accordance with FAR Part 10. The required system must be fully compatible with existing Revvity (PerkinElmer) JANUS platform components, including Modular Dispense Technology (MDT) hardware, integrated accessories, and WinPrep software protocols currently used by NCATS Compound Management. These systems are part of an established laboratory automation environment supporting ongoing research operations. Use of an alternative liquid handling system from another manufacturer would require significant modification or replacement of existing equipment, redevelopment and revalidation of protocols, and retraining of personnel. This would result in substantial duplication of cost to the Government and unacceptable delays to mission-critical research activities. Market research included a review of commercially available liquid handling systems, outreach to industry vendors, consultation with technical subject matter experts, and review of GSA Advantage, Dynamic Small Business Search, and prior Government acquisitions. While other liquid handling systems are available in the marketplace, none were identified that can meet the Government’s requirement for full compatibility with the existing JANUS-based infrastructure without significant additional cost and operational disruption. The JANUS G3 system is proprietary to its manufacturer, Revvity, Inc., and must be procured through a vendor capable of providing the required configuration, components, installation, and integration support. Based on market research, Copia Scientific is the only vendor identified that can provide the required system configuration and associated support necessary to meet the Government’s requirements within the required timeframe. Accordingly, this acquisition is justified under FAR 6.302-1.
