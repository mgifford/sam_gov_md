The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), Kansas City VA Medical Center (VAMC), 4801 Linwood Blvd., Kansas City, Missouri, is seeking information from capable and interested sources that can provide assisted medical transportation services (wheelchair and litter/cot) for eligible VA beneficiaries on a 24 hours per day, 7 days per week, 365 days per year basis. Services will include pick up and drop off of patients between the Kansas City VAMC and various locations such as residences, contract nursing homes, and non VA facilities, using safe, properly equipped vehicles operated by qualified drivers and attendants. The contemplated requirement is for a Firm Fixed Price Requirements contract with an Economic Price Adjustment provision based on the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) Midwest Region On Highway Diesel Fuel Price Index. Trip pricing will follow a structured rate methodology, including a flat base rate for one way trips within a 40 mile driving distance of the Kansas City VAMC and an additional mileage rate only for miles traveled beyond the 40 mile threshold, as calculated using Bing Maps quickest route. Contractors must be regularly established in the business of providing non emergent assisted medical transportation, possess sufficient vehicles, staff, and dispatch capability to meet variable demand, and comply with all applicable Federal, State, and local regulations. Anticipated performance requirements include maintaining 24/7 dispatch and response capability; meeting defined response times for scheduled and add on trips; ensuring proper patient securement; providing timely documentation (trip logs, no load events, waiting time, etc.); and supporting Government inspection and audit of vehicles, facilities, records, and operations. Interested sources must be able to demonstrate a compliant vehicle fleet (including safety inspections and equipment), trained and competent staff meeting VA, OSHA, infection control, and Joint Commission related competency and vaccination requirements (e.g., TB testing, Hepatitis B vaccination, bloodborne pathogen training); and adequate insurance coverage (general liability, auto liability, and workers compensation) as set by VA. Subcontracting of services for wheelchair and litter transports may be allowed only with prior VA approval and subject to the same qualifications and oversight standards.
