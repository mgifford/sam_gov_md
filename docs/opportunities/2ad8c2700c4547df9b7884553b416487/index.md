# Donor Kidney Facilitation Services

## Summary

General Information Document Type: Sources Sought Posted Date: March 4, 2026 Response due Date: March 13, 2026. Product or Service Code: Q523 � Medical Surgery Services NAICS Code: 621991 � Blood and Organ Banks The purpose of this Sources Sought is to conduct market research to determine if responsible sources exist, with the expertise, capabilities, and experience to successfully provide Donor Kidney Facilitation Services. The Defense Health Agency Contracting Activity (DHACA) on behalf of Walter Reed National Military Medical Center (WRNMMC) desires to procure Organ Transplant Services in support of the Department of Surgery at WRNMMC. This requirement is for the acquisition of kidney donor facilitation services in paired kidney donation/exchanges organized by the incumbent, between WRNMMC and the non-profit organization Alliance for Paired Kidney Donation (APKD). Scope of Work: The contractor is responsible for negotiating an agreement between WRNMMC and the Alliance for Paired Kidney Donation (APKD). The contractor is responsible for providing computerized match runs to find paired exchange matches and coordinate between medical centers to facilitate kidney transplant exchanges The contractor will offer the capability of cross-matching incompatible donor-recipient pairs with other member center patients in need of a kidney transplant with compatible living donors Contractors Shall Provide: Access to a secure website for entering necessary information for Incompatible Pairs and/or Non-directed Donors that come to Hospital through its transplant program. Identification of possible matches for Incompatible Pairs and/or Non-directed Donors. Coordination services for Kidney Paired Donation pairs from identification through completion of transplants. Arranging for collection of applicable SAC and payment of Covered Expenses in accordance with Section 5 when appropriate billing and approval procedures have been followed. Be advised that the U.S. Government will not be able to set aside this requirement if 2 or more small businesses do not respond with information to support the set-aside. We encourage all small businesses, in all socioeconomic categories (including, 8(a) Business Development Program, Small Disadvantage Business, Historically Underutilized Business Zone, Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned, Women-Owned Small Business concerns), to identify their capabilities in meeting the requirement at a fair market price.
