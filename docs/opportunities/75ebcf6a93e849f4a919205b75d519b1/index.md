DESCRIPTION: The Department of Veteran Affairs, Network Contracting Office 15 (NCO 15) is seeking sources that can provide phacoemulsification systems in accordance with (IAW) the below requirement and North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) code 339113 with a size standard of 800 employees. Kansas City VA Medical Center (KCVA) Surgical Services Ophthalmology department is requesting a phacoemulsification system for use in the OR. Phacoemulsification systems are specialized medical devices used in cataract surgery to emulsify and remove the eye's natural lens that has developed an opacification (clouding), which is commonly known as a cataract. General Scope of Work: This unit will be used for cataract surgery and most other intraocular procedures, including vitrectomy, and electrocautery in the OR. It can also be used for retinal surgeries. Salient Characteristics- Phacoemulsification system: Ultrasound Power Modulation: Ability to adjust the power and frequency of ultrasound waves to emulsify the lens efficiently and with minimal damage to surrounding tissues. Aspiration and Irrigation Systems: Efficient fluid management systems to maintain anterior chamber stability and to remove emulsified lens material while providing irrigation to keep the eye's structures cool and clear. User Interface: Intuitive and customizable user interfaces, typically featuring touch screens, foot pedals, and handpieces that allow for precise control during surgery. Safety Features: Integrated real-time monitoring of intraocular pressure, flow rates, and vacuum levels to prevent complications and ensure patient safety. Automatic shutdown or alert systems in case of irregularities. Energy Efficiency: Enhanced energy delivery systems to reduce the amount of heat generated within the eye, thereby minimizing the risk of thermal injury. Handpieces: Ergonomically designed, lightweight handpieces that facilitate precise movement and control. Multiple handpiece options for different surgical techniques and preferences. Programmable Settings: Customizable settings for different surgeons or different stages of the surgery, allowing for personalized operation. Compatibility: Compatibility with various surgical instruments, tips, and accessories that may be needed during different stages of the cataract surgery. Portability: Compact and portable design for easier transport and storage, especially in multi-operating room settings or mobile surgical units. Maintenance and Sterilization: Easy-to-clean components and materials that can be sterilized and maintained with minimal effort to ensure longevity and patient safety. Advanced Technologies: Integration of advanced technologies such as torsional or rotational ultrasound, or laser-assisted features for enhanced precision and outcomes. Training: If training is required it must be provided to the users within One (1) month of delivery and installation.
