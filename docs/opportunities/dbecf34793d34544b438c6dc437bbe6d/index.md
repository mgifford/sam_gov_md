# Z1DA--Improve Sub-Basement Plumbing and Air Quality - 541-24-103

- Posted: 2026-04-16 09:50:35.638-04

## Summary

Improve Sub-Basement Plumbing and Air Quality Project 541-24-103 This project replaces lift pumps, sump pumps, pits and other plumbing deficiencies in the sub-basement of the main medical center. Project also addresses air quality issues resulting from building expansion that has limited fresh air flow through the sub-basement. Ongoing projects have impeded egress, lighting and ventilation of the area. Contractor shall provide all labor, equipment, supplies, and supervision necessary for the Improve Sub-Basement Plumbing and Air Quality project at the Louis Stokes VA Medical Center (VAMC), 10701 East Boulevard, Cleveland, OH 44106. Period of Performance is 251 Calendar Days. The project magnitude for the project is between $10,000,000 and $20,000,000 The NAICS code for this project is 238220. The business size standard is $19 million. The Contractor Certification for VAAR 852.219-75 is required to be completed and submitted with the bid. Failure to provide the completed Contractor Certification for VAAR 852.219-75 shall deem non-responsive and the bid will not be considered. All work shall be coordinated with the Veteran Affairs (VA)Contracting Officer and the Contracting Officer's Representative (COR). All Bidders/Offerors shall insert bid(s) in the appropriate block(s) on the attached sheet titled "SF 1442 Block 17 Continuation on page 6." Bid(s) will be evaluated on price. Site Visit and place of performance is at Louis Stokes VA Medical Center (VAMC), 10701 East Boulevard, Cleveland, OH 44106. An organized site visit will be conducted – see solicitation attachment titled "SITE VISIT INFORMATION". Cut-off date and time to submit Requests for Information (RFI) is 04-30-2026 at 2:30 PM EST. RFIs shall be emailed to Stanley Burdine at stanley.burdine@va.gov. This project is a 100% set aside for Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business (SDVOSB). All bidders must be verified in VetCert and registered in SAM at the time of the offer due date and the time of award to be eligible for award. For eligibility under the NAICS, see VAAR 852.219.10 paragraph for performance requirements. Public Law 105-339, Section 1534 provides "no agency may obligate or expend funds appropriated for the agency for a fiscal year to enter a contract described in Section 4212(a) of Title 38 with a contractor from which a report was required under Section 4212(d) of that title with respect to the preceding fiscal year, if such contractor did not submit such report."
