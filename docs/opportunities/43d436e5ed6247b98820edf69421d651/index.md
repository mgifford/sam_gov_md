# Electromagnetic Spectrum (EMS) Services Applied Engineering, Spectrum, and Electromagnetic Environmental Effects (E3) Engineering Analysis Support

- Agency: DEPT OF DEFENSE
- Type: Solicitation
- Posted: 2026-04-07 13:31:05.114-04
- Solicitation Number: RFPHC104726R0015

## Summary

The Defense Information Systems Agency (DISA) has a requirement for Electromagnetic Spectrum (EMS) Services, Applied Engineering, and Spectrum and Electromagnetic Environmental Effects (E3) Engineering analysis support. This Request for Proposal is to solicit offers for this requirement. DISA Program Acquisition Executive (PAE) Transport requires technical advice, E3, and spectrum engineering support services. The E3 and spectrum engineer support services will facilitate the coordination of electromagnetic compatibility standards, analyze equipment and systems for potential E3 problems, recommend solutions, perform radio frequency measurements, develop and conduct E3 training, assist the acquisition community with E3 assessments, provide assistance in acquiring Host Nation approval for equipment operation, and evaluate frequency bands. The technical advice support services will include performing analyses on national and international EMS issues and providing technical advice to executive decision-makers on the technical viability of policy and implementation options. The Department of Defense (DoD) Instructions 4650.01 and 3222.03 require DISA to provide spectrum and E3 engineering analytical support services to programs developed or sustained by the DoD Components, Federal Agencies, Non- Federal Government organizations, and to foreign entities when authorized by the DoD Chief Information Officer, the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, or the Foreign Military Sales process through the Defense Security Cooperation Agency. Engineering support related to spectrum operations is an integral part of the overall information battlespace. It is essential that electromagnetic systems perform as expected to help achieve mission objectives in future operational environments. PROPOSAL DUE DATE: There are two phases for this solicitation.
