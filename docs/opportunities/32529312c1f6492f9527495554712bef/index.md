OBJECTIVE & REQUIREMENTS VA Medical Centers require a food service software system to automate Food Service Operations and Nutrition Care Management across four locations. The software shall be Windows-based, user-friendly, and feature point-and-click functionality with rapid data processing capabilities. General Requirements Operating Environment: Windows-based platform with user-friendly interface System Architecture: Preconfigured standalone interface ready for network integration IT Compliance: Single Sign-On capabilities and compliance with all VA IT requirements Integration Capability: Interface with VA VISTA System and Cerner Data Security: Unidirectional interface ensuring secure information transfer without risk of data overwriting, security breaches, or VISTA system corruption 2. FOOD SERVICE OPERATIONS MANAGEMENT The system shall provide comprehensive food service management functionality:

2.1 Recipe & Inventory Management Manage standardized ingredients and scalable recipes Support multiple menu types simultaneously (Day Care, CLC, Room Service, etc.) Perpetual inventory capability with tracking by day, week, month, year, vendor, storage location, or inventory group Ability to transfer items between inventory locations Real-time inventory updates with forecasted item requirements

2.2 Menu Planning & Production Real-time transfer of Admission, Discharge, Transfer, and diet order information from VISTA Production tally sheets with patient-specific substitutions and date-specific nourishments Define batch, pan, or distribution size for production needs Automated HACCP compliance tracking Support for product recalls with ability to globally locate and change items in recipes and menus for specific date ranges or permanently

2.3 Ordering & Purchasing Automate ordering and inventory processes Integrated electronic ordering and confirmation system Automated vendor price updates Generate backup purchase orders based on on-hand inventory, on-order goods, and committed products Ability to calculate year-to-date information by product category

2.4 Reporting & Analytics Comprehensive filtering and customizable reports Ability to view reports on-screen, print, or export to Excel, PDF, HTML, or graphics formats Save report settings for future use Pre-cost and post-cost capabilities for financial analysis Cost controls and production forecasting tools

2.5 Nutrient Database & Allergen Management Access to USDA nutrient database for generic foods Vendor-supplied database including manufacturer, distributor, and branded item information Ability to add new nutrient data for ingredients as information becomes available Link recipes to ingredients, nutritional content, and potential allergens Allergen blocking functionality to prevent accidental allergen selection during patient menu selection 3. NUTRITION CARE MANAGEMENT The system shall track and manage patient nutritional needs from admission to discharge:

3.1 Patient Tracking & Monitoring Track patient diet orders and diet order changes Monitor weight changes and identify trends Identify patient allergies, likes, dislikes, and demographic data Track preferred meal patterns and diet order preferences Automatic meal pattern assignment based on nutritional needs

3.2 Nutritional Assessment & Calculations Automatic calculation of Basal Energy Expenditure (BEE) Automatic calculation of Body Mass Index (BMI) Automatic calculation of protein requirements Weight tracking and reporting capabilities Nutrition notes and comprehensive nutrient analysis Calorie counting reports with carbohydrate counting functionality

3.3 Menu & Tray Management Create patient-specific meal patterns Produce on-demand menus and tray tickets Ensure foods to which a patient is allergic never print on tray tickets, kitchen tickets, or nourishment labels Bedside data and menu entry via wireless services Comprehensive nutrient analysis and calorie counts per meal

3.4 Dietary Management Tube feeding management and associated diet orders Track likes, dislikes, and allergies Medical history documentation Patient-specific diet order modifications Automated safety controls preventing allergen selection errors 4. VA VISTA & CERNER INTERFACE CAPABILITIES The system shall provide seamless integration with VA clinical systems: Real-time Data Transfer: Admission, discharge, transfer, and diet order information transfer in real-time from VISTA to the new system Unidirectional Interface: Secure transfer of information from VISTA to the new system without risk of data overwriting, security breaches, or data corruption HL7 Compliance: Multi-Site HL7 ADT/DO with Tube Feeding Interface for comprehensive clinical data integration Single Sign-On: Integrated Single Sign-On authentication aligned with VA IT security requirements Cerner Compatibility: Ready for integration with Cerner systems 5. CUSTOMER SUPPORT & SERVICES The vendor shall provide the following support services:

5.1 Account Management Dedicated account manager available for troubleshooting software issues Initial implementation and system configuration support

5.2 User Support Support line available during standard business hours Ability for end-users to submit enhancement requests for software improvements Technical assistance for system operation and optimization

5.3 System Maintenance Regular updates and maintenance for system compatibility Ongoing vendor support for system optimization 6. POSSIBLE LOCATIONS & SCOPE This SOW applies to the following VA Medical Centers: San Diego VA Medical Center Long Beach VA Medical Center (Primary Site) Greater Los Angeles VA Medical Center Loma Linda VA Medical Center Albuquerque VA Medical Center Northern AZ VA Medical Center Pheonix VA Medical Center Southern AZ VA Medical Center The software shall serve all Nutrition and Food Service departments at these locations
