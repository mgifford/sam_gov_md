# Tactical Tomahawk Maritime Strike Supply Chain Management Data Deliverables

- Agency: DEPT OF DEFENSE
- Type: Presolicitation
- Posted: 2026-03-19 10:46:03.507-04
- Solicitation Number: N00019-26-RFPREQ-WPM280-0177

## Summary

The Naval Air Systems Command (NAVAIR) intends to negotiate and award a contract modification in support of the Maritime Strike Tomahawk (MST) to procure supply chain management data deliverables. The supplier and material data collected is critical to not only illuminate Weapons Industrial Base, but will support modeling and forecasting of demand, capacity, and capability of the industrial base. It will enable efficient aggregation of common materials and their sources across PEO Weapons for promotion of critical material needs and develop mitigates supply base disruptions. The period of performance is anticipated to be up to two years. Contract award is anticipated in Third Quarter Fiscal Year (FY) 2026. This requirement supports the production and delivery of MST-capable TACTOM Block V / Va AUR missiles in quantities as authorized and appropriated by Congress. MST-related production activities will remain aligned with ongoing TACTOM recertification and modernization efforts to ensure efficient integration and delivery. NAVAIR intends to award this effort to Raytheon, P.O. Box 11337, Tucson, AZ 85734-1337, as the only responsible source with the requisite technical data, manufacturing capability, and production experience necessary to execute the MST procure supply chain management data deliverables within the required schedule and without unacceptable duplication of cost. (Point of Contact for Small Businesses interested in subcontracting: Patrick Marchant, Phone: 520-392-0435, Email: Patrick.J.Marchant@rtx.com) History: Since 1994, Raytheon has been the sole developer, designer, and manufacturer for the Tomahawk Land Attack Missile. Raytheon solely possesses the current configuration and manufacturing data for the Tomahawk Block IV AUR missile, the seeker suite, and the installation of the seeker suites into the Block IV missiles during recertification in order to deliver a Block Va TACTOM AUR. They are the only source capable of performing the required integration and testing. Raytheon maintains and controls both the Level III TDP and the manufacturing documentation for the Block IV AUR as well as the Level III TDP for the Seeker Kit, and the TDP for the Block Va AUR. Because a detailed knowledge and expertise of the Tomahawk Block IV, V, & Va AUR missile is required to conduct tasking under this contract, Raytheon is the only known source capable of accomplishing this requirement in the required timeframe and without duplication of costs. The data associated with the Tomahawk missile is export controlled and is not available to foreign sources or representatives. Responsible parties may submit capability statements which shall be considered. This is not a request for proposals.
