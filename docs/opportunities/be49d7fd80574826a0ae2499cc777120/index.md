SCOPE OF WORK: The Veterans Health Administration (VHA) Non-Expendable Equipment National Program has identified the Surgical Tissue Management System product line as a candidate for a VA-wide (otherwise referred to as national ) Requirements contract. The Surgical Tissue Management System is an advanced, energy-based surgical platform used across Veterans Affairs Medical Centers (VAMCs) in operating rooms, procedural suites, and endoscopy units to perform precise tissue coagulation, devitalization, cutting, and vessel sealing. The system delivers radiofrequency (RF) energy and ultrasonic energy through specialized electrodes and hand instruments to support uniform, noncontact coagulation with minimal tissue penetration, improved visualization, and reduced bleeding. Units that provide both radio frequency power to drive electrosurgical instruments and mechanical vibrations to power ultrasonic surgical instruments used in the process of joining or sealing blood vessels together. The unit typically features receptacle ports, various output settings, power levels, and control mechanisms to adjust the energy delivered to the surgical site. It may also have features such as audible alarms, power monitoring, and temperature regulation. This universal platform provides multiple energy modalities (ultrasonic, bipolar, and RF) to support a broad range of open, laparoscopic, and endoscopic procedures in general surgery, urology, and gynecology, helping reduce the number of separate generators and instruments required in the operating room. It is designed to enhance surgical efficiency, improve hemostasis, and support consistent, replicable outcomes across diverse clinical applications The primary hand instrument is a hybrid ultrasonic and bipolar electrosurgical device intended for use in open and laparoscopic procedures to cut, seal, coagulate, and dissect with two distinct modes of action: Seal & Cut and Seal Only. It is indicated for ligation and division of vessels (up to and including 7 mm), lymphatics, and tissue bundles, and may be used in minimally invasive or open surgical procedures where vessel sealing and tissue division are desired. The device is intended for single use only and has not been shown to be effective for tubal sterilization or tubal coagulation for sterilization procedures. The Contractor must demonstrate the ability to meet all requirements for the solicitation. The objective is to provide Surgical Tissue Management Systems to be used by clinicians throughout the VA medical centers and facilities. The period of performance is for a base of 12-months with four 12-month option periods. 3.
